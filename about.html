About PSP Association

Payment Service Providers’ Association of Nepal is a not-for-profit, independent, umbrella organisation formed by the combined efforts of (PSPs), digital wallets of Nepal. We are committed to building a strong, united community of payment service providers through cooperation, knowledge-sharing, and a shared vision for the digital future. Our focus lies in upholding the advocacy for our members while promoting access to technology and innovation. We work closely with government bodies and stakeholders to provide policy input, regulatory feedback, and technical insights that help shape a fair and inclusive digital financial environment. We also prioritise fostering a culture of continuous learning by organising know-how sharing sessions, workshops, and events that keep members updated on the latest trends and technologies in digital finance. By empowering our members with knowledge and skills, we help them stay relevant in a rapidly digital landscape.

Our Objective

Promote unity among payment service providers

Advocate for supportive policies and regulations

Raise digital literacy and awareness

Encourage research and innovation

Support youth skill development and training

Together, we aim to drive Nepal’s economic growth by strengthening the digital payment landscape and supporting the broader vision of a digitally empowered nation.

Member Organizations

Our member organizations are leading payment service providers in Nepal, driving innovation and financial inclusion through secure and accessible digital payment solutions.

Cell Pay

CellPay is a Nepal Rastra Bank licensed Payment Service Provider providing both bank link and wallet facility from the same app. CellPay is a mobile application that has been uniquely placed with a dual feature of a digital wallet and a bank link facility, easing customer needs.

CityPay

CityPay is a licensed digital wallet in Nepal, developed by City Wallet Pvt. Ltd. and promoted by City Express. It offers secure mobile payments, utility bill services, remittance payouts, and QR transactions, aiming to enhance financial accessibility and convenience nationwide.

Visit Website

Hamro Pay

Hamro Pay is a licensed digital wallet launched by Hamro Patro to provide secure and convenient digital payment services in Nepal. It offers mobile top-ups, utility bill payments, remittance, and QR-based transactions. Backed by Nepal Rastra Bank, Hamro Pay aims to promote financial inclusion through innovative and accessible digital solutions.

Visit Website

IME Pay

IME Pay is a licensed digital wallet and payment service provider in Nepal, offering a wide range of services including money transfers, utility bill payments, QR code transactions, and remittance services. With a vast network of over 30,000 agents and 15,000 merchants, IME Pay is committed to enhancing financial inclusion and providing convenient digital financial solutions across the country.

Visit Website

Khalti

Khalti is a licensed digital wallet and payment gateway in Nepal, offering a wide range of online services including mobile top-ups, utility bill payments, domestic flight bookings, and event ticketing. Launched in 2017, it has become a trusted platform for over 15 million users nationwide. Khalti is authorized by Nepal Rastra Bank and is committed to promoting financial inclusion through secure and user-friendly digital solutions.

Visit Website

MOCO

MOCO is a licensed digital wallet in Nepal by FOCUSONE Payment Solutions, offering seamless in-app and QR payments by linking multiple Visa and Mastercard cards. It supports utility bill payments, mobile top-ups, flight bookings, and cross-border transactions through its unified QR code system, integrating networks like Alipay+, UnionPay, and NEPALPAY.

Visit Website

Moru

Moru is a licensed digital wallet in Nepal offering services like mobile top-ups, bill payments, flight bookings, and QR transactions. It supports bank transfers and card loading, providing cashback and 24/7 support. Authorized by Nepal Rastra Bank, Moru promotes digital payments and financial inclusion.

Visit Website

MyPay

MyPay is a licensed digital wallet in Nepal, developed by Smart Card Nepal Pvt. Ltd., offering secure and convenient financial services. Users can perform mobile top-ups, utility bill payments, fund transfers, and book travel tickets, including bus and cable car reservations. MyPay also facilitates DEMAT renewals, remittance services, and government payments, aiming to enhance digital financial inclusion across the country.

Visit Website

Prabhu Pay

PrabhuPAY is a licensed digital wallet in Nepal, developed by Prabhu Technology Pvt. Ltd., offering a wide range of financial services including utility bill payments, fund transfers, ticket bookings, and remittance. With features like QR payments, bank linking, and cashback rewards, it simplifies everyday transactions for users across the country.
